BFA 2028

Tianyu Zhang is an Artist whose interdisciplinary practice spans painting, sculpture, music, games, and interactive installations. Her work explores technology and social ethics, food and human health, and the relationship between people and nature.

↗ tzhang08@risd.edu

I was born and raised in Zhuhai, Guangdong Province, China. I've loved drawing since I was a child—art has always been an essential way for me to express myself. But before I turned 18, my life was relatively suppressed. The education system I grew up in didn't really recognize my forms of creative expression. The unspoken rule everyone accepted was that we all had to compete on the same track—with no room for failure. Interpersonal relationships were also difficult for me, and my mental state hit rock bottom during that period. If everyone is the same, then who am I?

Rap music profoundly influenced me in high school. I was fascinated by its storytelling and free-flowing arrangements—I saw an entirely new form of self-expression, a kind of possibility with no correct answers. Around the same time, I began a process of psychological self-healing. It lasted a year and a half and was painful, but it was the most extraordinary experience of my life—my entire way of thinking and perceiving was turned upside down. I felt as though my past self and I were two different people: we would make completely opposite decisions and have completely opposite experiences of the same thing. I began to know who I was.

In 2022, under the impact of the pandemic, the entrance exam I had registered for at the Academy of Arts & Design, Tsinghua University, was postponed to mid-April. With the gaokao (China's national college entrance exam) just over a month away and my review not yet started, I didn't hold out much hope. But when the results came out, I had scored first in all of Guangdong Province—and 16th in the entire country—on Tsinghua's art examination. Even I was stunned. In the end, I didn't get into Tsinghua because I scored only 46 on the math section of the gaokao; if I had reached 60, I would be holding a Tsinghua diploma right now. But those exams and scores feel very distant from who I am today.

After graduating from high school, I began learning music production, trying to translate my ideas into audio tracks and effects. At RISD, I started experimenting with expressing my life experiences and reflections across various media. At its core, my work is about intimate relationships—my relationship with the world, with food, with myself, with nature, and with society.
