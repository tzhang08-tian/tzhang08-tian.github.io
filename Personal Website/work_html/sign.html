Material: PenPlotter, Mulberry Paper, Ink

Size: 5cm x 10cm

This work was drawn by a machine on mulberry paper. I act as a threshold, controlling the computer's input and changing the types of pens used throughout the process.

Beneath Ableton's impeccably ordered red interface lies a landscape of emotional disorder. Different layers interfere with and bleed into one another: the irregular strokes of pencil interrupt the absorption of red ink, while black marker spreads across the paper, obscuring and overtaking the red.

The inner world of contemporary individuals resembles this interface—structured, yet constantly vulnerable to disruption. We continuously test the possibility of crossing boundaries from within systems of rules, while simultaneously exercising self-control and experiencing moments of collapse.
