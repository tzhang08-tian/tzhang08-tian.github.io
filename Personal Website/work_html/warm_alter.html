Speaking of Death

When we speak of death, it seems to exist only in monochrome.
Death is solemn, and commemoration is reverent.
But when I recall my grandparents' faces, I feel it's not quite so.
Just as the furniture we place in our homes carries the owner's beloved aesthetic and warmth,
an altar too should carry the warmth of family.
I hope that each time on their death anniversaries and the Double Ninth Festival, when I see their photographs,
my heart feels warm, joyful, full of longing—
just like the smiles on their faces in those images.

The Work

I took photographs of my grandfather from his lifetime and transformed them into negative film.
Using cyanotype technique, I remade them as blue reproductions.
Cut and collaged, I fixed them onto foam board with colored pins—
the way we might want to pin the beautiful moments of our daily lives onto a wall to hold them there.
